Breath is the bridge between body and mind. In yoga, we call breathwork **pranayama** ‚Äì the practice of controlling the life force (prana) through conscious breathing.

## Why Breathing Matters

Most of us breathe shallowly, using only a fraction of our lung capacity. This leads to:

- Reduced oxygen intake
- Increased stress and anxiety
- Poor energy levels
- Disconnection from the present moment

Through pranayama, we can transform our relationship with breath and, by extension, with life itself.

## Three Essential Techniques

### 1. Ujjayi Breath (Ocean Breath)

Often called "victorious breath," ujjayi creates a soft hissing sound by slightly constricting the throat.

**Benefits:**
- Calms the nervous system
- Increases focus during asana practice
- Builds internal heat

**How to practice:**
1. Inhale deeply through your nose
2. Slightly constrict the back of your throat
3. Exhale slowly, creating a gentle ocean-like sound
4. Continue for 5-10 minutes

### 2. Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing)

This balancing technique purifies the energy channels (nadis) and harmonizes the left and right hemispheres of the brain.

**Benefits:**
- Reduces stress and anxiety
- Improves mental clarity
- Balances emotions
- Prepares for meditation

**How to practice:**
1. Sit comfortably with spine straight
2. Use your right thumb to close right nostril
3. Inhale through left nostril
4. Close left nostril with ring finger
5. Release right nostril and exhale
6. Inhale through right, switch, exhale through left
7. Continue for 5-10 rounds

### 3. Kapalabhati (Skull Shining Breath)

A powerful cleansing breath that energizes and detoxifies.

**Benefits:**
- Cleanses respiratory system
- Increases energy
- Strengthens core
- Clears mental fog

**How to practice:**
1. Take a deep inhale
2. Exhale forcefully through nose by contracting abdomen
3. Allow passive inhale
4. Repeat 20-30 times
5. Take 3 rounds with rest between

## Integrating Breathwork Into Your Practice

Start your yoga session with 5-10 minutes of pranayama. This:

- Prepares your body for asana
- Calms your mind
- Establishes present-moment awareness
- Sets an intention for practice

## Safety Considerations

**Important reminders:**
- Never force the breath
- Stop if you feel dizzy or uncomfortable
- Avoid intense pranayama during pregnancy
- Practice on an empty stomach
- Learn from a qualified teacher first

## Your Breath, Your Life

As the ancient texts remind us: *"Life is in the breath; therefore he who only half breathes, half lives."*

Take time each day to connect with your breath. Start with just 5 minutes. Notice how it transforms not just your yoga practice, but your entire day.
