Transform your practice. Inspire others.

Discover Aerial Yoga

Aerial yoga combines traditional yoga postures with the support of a silk hammock suspended from the ceiling. This unique practice allows you to explore poses from a new perspectiveâ€”floating, inverting, and stretching in ways that ground-based yoga simply cannot offer. The hammock supports your body weight, making challenging poses more accessible while deepening stretches and building core strength.

Our 100-hour certification program goes beyond the physical practice. You'll learn the art of teaching aerial yoga safely and effectively, understanding body mechanics, rigging fundamentals, and how to guide students of all levels through transformative aerial sequences. Whether you're a yoga teacher looking to expand your offerings or a dedicated practitioner ready to share your passion, this training provides the foundation you need.

What You'll Learn

  • History and evolution of aerial yoga
  • Physical, mental, and emotional benefits
  • Contraindications and precautions
  • Hammock setup (types of hammocks)
  • Basic aerial yoga postures (manual)
  • Hammock familiarization: Joint grips, fabric height (low-medium-high), posture adjustments and variations, open and closed fabric
  • Using the hammock for spinal decompression
  • Specific therapeutic applications for each posture
  • Fabric entries and exits
  • Yamas and Niyamas
  • Chakras and Nadis
  • Koshas
  • Patanjali's Yoga Sutras
  • Joints
  • Major muscles
  • Fascia
  • The nervous system
  • The endocrine system
  • Deep muscles: Bandhas and aerial Pilates
  • Advanced inversions and progressions
  • Introduction to drops (controlled falls)
  • Spins
  • Advanced wraps (wrapping techniques)
  • Progressions from basic to advanced postures
  • Stretches, hip opening, backbends, twists
  • Vinyasa: Sun and moon salutations
  • How to design a balanced sequence
  • Principles of aerial movement fluidity
  • Variations and dynamics
  • Safe posture transitions
  • Aerial Yoga Nidra
  • Deep relaxation sequences
  • The importance of breath in yoga and meditation
  • Breathwork exercises (pranayama)
  • The therapeutic qualities of sound
  • Throat opening and mantra practice
  • The art of meditation
  • Aerial yoga applications
  • How to lead a class: Voice use, projection, rhythm, clarity, and body language
  • Assistance and adjustments in aerial yoga

  • Adaptations for people with physical limitations
